## ðŸ§¬ The 8-Section Framework

### The Pattern (AIDA-Optimized)

1. **HOOK** â†’ Attention: Dream outcome (hero image + price)
2. **PROOF** â†’ Interest: Social validation (reviews + customer photos)
3. **HOW** â†’ Interest: Remove complexity (3-step process)
4. **WHO** â†’ Desire: Emotional connection (4 personas carousel)
5. **WHAT** â†’ Desire: Feature clarity (3 gear categories carousel)
6. **CHOOSE** â†’ Action: Selection moment (vehicle showcase + CTA)
7. **TRUST** â†’ Action: Objection handling (top 3 FAQs)
8. **ACTION** â†’ Action: Final conversion (CTA + urgency)

### Why 8 Sections?

- **Psychological sweet spot**: Enough depth to build trust, not overwhelming
- **Zeigarnik effect**: Progress indicator creates "must finish" tension
- **Progressive commitment**: Each swipe is a micro-commitment (sunk cost)
- **Serial position effect**: First and last sections remembered most

## ðŸ’¡ The Innovation: Progressive Disclosure

**Problem**: 15 desktop content segments â†’ How to fit in 8 mobile sections?

**Solution**: Horizontal carousels within vertical scroll

### Pattern Rules:
- **Vertical scroll** = Progress through journey (AIDA flow)
- **Horizontal swipe** = Explore variations (personas, gear, vehicles)
- **One gesture per concept** = Instant understanding

### Real Example:
**Before**: 4 separate overview screens (Drive, Camp, Wake, Explore)
**After**: 1 "WHO" section with 4-item carousel
**Result**: 75% reduction in scroll depth, 0% content loss

## ðŸ§  Why This Works (Psychology)

### 1. Recognition Over Recall

### 2. Zeigarnik Effect
